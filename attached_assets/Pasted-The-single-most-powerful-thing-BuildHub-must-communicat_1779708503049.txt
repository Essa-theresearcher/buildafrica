The single most powerful thing BuildHub must communicate to every 
company or client who visits is this:

PROOF OF WORK — NOT PROOF OF TALK

When a client lands on BuildHub looking for a builder, they are 
carrying one fear:

"I will pay someone, they will disappear, ghost me, or deliver 
something broken."

That fear is why they go to Toptal and pay $150/hour.
That fear is why they post on Upwork and wade through 200 bids.
That fear is why they ask their WhatsApp contacts instead of 
using a platform.

BuildHub kills that fear not with promises — but with evidence.

WHAT PROOF OF WORK LOOKS LIKE ON BUILDHUB
==========================================

When a client views a builder profile they must immediately see:

1. SHIPPED PRODUCTS — not "projects I worked on"
   Real launched products with live demo links, screenshots, 
   and exactly what this builder contributed. Not team projects 
   where their role is unclear. Specific. Attributed. Verifiable.

2. STATUS TAGS THAT MEAN SOMETHING
   - "Verified Builder" = admin has reviewed and approved this person
   - "Shipped Project" = has at least one live launched product on record
   - "Reliable Collaborator" = other builders have worked with them 
     and confirmed they delivered
   - "Available for Work" = actively looking right now
   - "Coffee & Code Member" = part of a known trusted community
   
   These tags are earned, not self-assigned.
   A builder cannot give themselves "Verified Builder."
   An admin grants it after review.
   This is what makes the tag mean something.

3. COLLABORATION HISTORY — not just solo projects
   Has this builder worked with other people and delivered?
   Were they on a team? What did they specifically build?
   This answers: "Can they work with my team or just alone?"

4. TECH STACK WITH RECEIPTS
   Not a list of buzzwords like on LinkedIn.
   Every skill claim is backed by a project that used it.
   If a builder says they know React, there must be a shipped 
   React project visible on their profile. No project = no tag.

5. PROJECT STATUS HONESTY
   Every project has a status: Idea / Building / Launched / Verified
   Clients can filter by "Launched" or "Verified" only.
   This means a builder cannot pad their profile with 
   half-finished side projects to look more impressive.
   Honesty is built into the structure.

THE FEATURE THAT MAKES THIS REAL
==================================

The Admin Reliability Layer.

This is what no other platform has for East Africa.

When a company submits a request, the admin does not just 
send a list of profiles. The admin adds private notes:

"Omar delivered the inventory module 2 days early."
"Amina communicated clearly throughout the project."
"Ibrahim went quiet for a week mid-project — flag for follow-up."

These notes are not public. But they power the matching.
The admin only matches builders with a clean reliability record.

This means when a company gets a match from BuildHub, 
they are not getting a random bidder.
They are getting someone a human being has personally 
vouched for based on real execution history.

That is the product.
That is what Upwork cannot sell.
That is what Toptal charges $150/hour to approximate.
BuildHub does it from inside the community — at community cost.

1. What has this person actually shipped?

3. What did other people say about working with them?
4. Are they available right now?
5. How do I contact them directly?

If the profile page cannot answer all 5 questions
