You are building BuildHub — a trusted builder discovery platform for East Africa.

CONTEXT: THE GAP YOU ARE FILLING
==================================

Right now in East Africa, three problems exist simultaneously:

PROBLEM 1 — BUILDERS ARE INVISIBLE
Developers in Nairobi, Mogadishu, Kampala, and Addis Ababa are shipping 
real products every week. Fintech tools. School management systems. 
Logistics dashboards. Inventory SaaS apps. They built them. They launched 
them. They work. But nobody outside their WhatsApp group ever sees them. 
There is no structured place to say "I built this, it works, here is proof."

PROBLEM 2 — COMPANIES CANNOT FIND TRUSTED BUILDERS
When a company in Nairobi or a startup in Mogadishu needs a reliable 
developer, they have no trusted local discovery layer. They either:
- Post on Upwork and get 200 random bids from around the world
- Ask friends on WhatsApp and hire whoever responds first
- Use Toptal or Arc.dev which cost $60-200/hour and are built for 
  Silicon Valley budgets, not East African startups
- Use LinkedIn which rewards connections and talking, not actual code

PROBLEM 3 — THERE IS NO PROOF-OF-WORK LAYER
Existing platforms reward:
- How well you write your CV (LinkedIn)
- How cheap you bid (Upwork, Fiverr)
- How well you pass a 5-stage Western interview (Toptal)

None of them answer the only question that actually matters:
"Has this person shipped real working products and delivered reliably?"


THE COMPETITORS AND EXACTLY WHY THEY FAIL EAST AFRICAN BUILDERS
=================================================================

UPWORK
- Global race to the bottom on price
- East African builders compete against developers from everywhere
- No community trust, no verification of actual shipped products
- Builders are anonymous bidders, not recognized professionals
- Zero proof-of-work — a builder with 10 shipped products looks 
  identical to someone who has never shipped anything
- Companies get overwhelmed with bids, trust nobody
- KEY FEATURE GAP: Upwork has zero concept of "shipped product portfolio"
  It only shows ratings from past Upwork jobs — if you never used Upwork 
  before, you are invisible with zero credibility even if you have built 
  10 real products

TOPTAL / ARC.DEV / LEMON.IO
- Minimum $60-200/hour, built for US/European enterprise budgets
- 5-stage vetting process takes weeks
- Zero focus on East Africa, no cultural or community context
- Builders must pass Western interview formats to even be listed
- Companies must pay subscription fees just to search

LINKEDIN
- Rewards connections and self-promotion, not execution
- No way to verify "I built this product and it works"
- Full of people who say they are developers without shipped proof
- East African builders with no network connections stay invisible
- No structured way for companies to post "I need a builder" 
  and get matched to verified local talent

ANDELA / GEBEYA
- Corporate, top-down, they choose who gets on the platform
- Builders cannot self-list — they must be selected and trained
- Built for placing talent at global corporations, not local startups
- No community-native feel, no proof-of-work showcase
- East African startup founder cannot use these to find a local builder

GITHUB
- Shows code, not products
- Companies cannot browse "builders available for hire in Nairobi"
- No collaboration history, no availability status, no trust signals
- Not designed for discovery


WHAT BUILDHUB IS
================

BuildHub is the trust layer that does not exist yet.

It is NOT a job board.
It is NOT a freelance bidding marketplace.
It is NOT a social network.
It is NOT a generic portfolio builder.

It IS a verified builder discovery platform where:
- Builders create profiles showing real shipped products with screenshots,
  demo links, tech stack, and what they personally contributed
- Every builder has a verification status (Verified Builder, Shipped 
  Project, Reliable Collaborator, Available for Work, Coffee & Code Member)
- Companies submit exactly what they need and get matched to 
  verified builders who have proof they can deliver
- An admin layer maintains trust by approving builders and 
  adding reliability notes — this is the trust engine

THE SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT FEATURE DIFFERENCE FROM UPWORK
=========================================================

On Upwork: a builder's credibility comes from Upwork job ratings.
If you never used Upwork before = zero credibility, even if you 
built 10 real shipped products.

On BuildHub: a builder's credibility comes from SHIPPED PRODUCTS.
A builder with zero clients but 5 live launched products with 
screenshots, demo links, and tech stack is MORE credible than 
someone with 50 Upwork jobs and no portfolio of real products.

This is the core design principle. Every feature must reinforce this.
Proof of execution beats proof of platform activity. Always.


COMMUNITY CONTEXT
=================

BuildHub starts with the Coffee & Code community in Nairobi/Somalia.
This is not a cold-start platform. It has a real community behind it.
The first builders are people who already know each other, already 
ship products, and already trust each other. The platform formalizes 
and amplifies that existing trust — it does not try to manufacture it.


WHAT YOU ARE BUILDING — V1 SCOPE
=================================

Pages:
1. Landing page — explains the gap, calls builders and companies to action
2. Builders Directory — browse verified builders by skill, location, availability
3. Builder Profile — name, location, role, skills, shipped projects, 
   collaboration history, availability, verification tags, contact button
4. Project Showcase — project name, description, builder, tech stack, 
   status (Idea/Building/Launched/Verified), demo link, what builder contributed
5. Company Request Form — company posts what they need, budget, timeline, 
   remote or local, contact email
6. Admin Dashboard — approve builders, view company requests, 
   match builders to companies, add reliability notes

Tech stack:
- React frontend
- Tailwind CSS
- Supabase for database and auth
- Mobile responsive
- Dark, minimal, serious, professional design
- No loud gamification, no leaderboards, no fake social feed

Reputation signals (simple tags only, no AI scoring yet):
- Verified Builder
- Shipped Project  
- Reliable Collaborator
- Available for Work
- Coffee & Code Member

Seed data: Nairobi/Somali builders and companies with real-feeling 
dummy data — InventoryPro, HireMatch AI, SchoolFlow, CargoTrack, 
ClipCraft as sample projects. Builders named Omar Issa, Amina Ali, 
Ibrahim Hassan, Fadumo Noor, Yahye Mohamed.

DESIGN PRINCIPLE FOR EVERY SCREEN
===================================

Every screen must answer one question for the person looking at it:
"Can this person actually build and deliver reliably?"

If a feature does not help answer that question, do not build it yet.
